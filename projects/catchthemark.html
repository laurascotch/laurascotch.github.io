Catch the mark competition - 2020

Competition for teams of students attending the course Multimedia Data Security (MSc in Computer Science, UniTN, 2020/'21)

The competition

The Professor organizes this competition every year as part of the students' evaluation.
The game is composed of two phases: in the first one the groups are given some grayscale images and the students must embed a watermark maximizing its robustness and minimizing the quality loss of the images. Teams are free to choose whatever embedding algorithm they prefer, given that the watermarked images are delivered within the time limit.
The second part is the attack phase. Every team must attack as many watermarked images as possible from other teams: the goal is to destroy the watermark without damaging too much the quality of the image. The function detecting if the watermark is still present is provided by the attacked team(s).

Points were assigned for each phase based on various criteria (e.g. quality, activity, etc.).

My team (whatamark - read as "What a mark!!) won the competition by developing an embedding algorithm based on multiple applications of the discrete wavelet transform.
